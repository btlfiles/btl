16' (deck-to-fence) x 33' (existing + expansion) / 4 Cups / All Putt Lines Clear

Cup Position

Putt Line / Distance

4 Cups / Every Putt Line Clear

No cup sits in the path between any other two cups. You can putt from any hole to any hole without interference.

1
Existing / Fence Side (6' in, 4' from fence)
Near the house, fence side. Your short-game anchor. 11' diagonal to Cup 2, 18' straight shot to Cup 3.
2
Existing / Deck Side (14' in, 4' from deck)
Opposite corner of the existing green. Closest to where you step off the deck. 12' diagonal to Cup 3, 14' to Cup 4.
3
Expansion / Fence Side (24' in, 5' from fence)
Into the new section, fence side. Short 7' putt to Cup 4. Long 18' from Cup 1. Mirrors Cup 1's position in the expansion half.
4
Expansion / Deck Side (28' in, 5' from deck)
Farthest from the house. 23' from Cup 1 is your longest putt. 7' from Cup 3 for quick reps near the grate.
Why 4 cups on 528 sq ft: Course practice greens run about 1 cup per 100-150 sq ft. 4 cups gives you 132 sq ft per hole, which is right in that sweet spot. Every putt lane between any two cups is completely clear of other holes. You get 6 unique putt combinations ranging from 7' to 23'. The offset pattern (cups zigzag fence-side to deck-side) means you're always changing your line and distance. No two putts feel the same.
